14 — Do's & Don'ts

Protect the brand
at every touchpoint.

Every brand decision is a signal. These guidelines distill the most important boundaries — the commitments that keep the brand coherent, credible, and distinct from noise in a saturated market.

Visual Do's

  • Use Deep Tech Blue as the primary surface for hero sections and covers
  • Apply Electric Cyan sparingly — for maximum signal value
  • Maintain generous whitespace — density is intentional, not default
  • Use architecture diagrams and system visualizations as imagery
  • Pair Syne display with Space Grotesk body text consistently
  • Use the dark logo on Deep Blue, light logo on white/grey surfaces
  • Maintain logo clear space equal to the "M" character height

Visual Don'ts

  • Don't use colorful gradients, purple tones, or playful palette additions
  • Don't use generic stock photography of servers, handshakes, or clouds
  • Don't use decorative icons or illustration styles from marketing toolkits
  • Don't add drop shadows, glow effects, or 3D treatments to the logo
  • Don't compress or stretch the logo proportions
  • Don't use Arial, Roboto, or system fonts in brand materials
  • Don't crowd layouts — negative space is a design statement

Voice Do's

  • Lead with the problem, then the principle, then the outcome
  • Use specific, concrete language — "40% delivery slowdown" not "slower delivery"
  • Acknowledge that early architectural decisions made under pressure were rational
  • Position architecture as a strategic business lever, not a technical cost
  • Speak from the perspective of someone who has built real systems at real scale
  • Use "engineering velocity," "cloud unit economics," "blast radius" as precision terms

Voice Don'ts

  • Don't use AI hype language: "revolutionary," "game-changing," "disruptive"
  • Don't lead with tooling (Kubernetes, Terraform, GCP) before context
  • Don't use "consultant" or "freelancer" to describe the engagement model
  • Don't suggest that clients don't understand their own problems
  • Don't make vague promises — every claim must be grounded in outcomes
  • Don't use "Principal" as a public title — it is an internal level descriptor
